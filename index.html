Ultra Running

Training

It is important to train your body to go long distances, but you
don't want to overtrain and risk injury. Most ultra-runners recommend
running 4-6 times per week. One of these runs should be a long run
(eventually building up to 30 miles). The rest should be moderate distance
runs (anywhere from 5-10 miles).

Here is a sample training plan for one week:

Monday Tuesday Wednesday Thursday Friday Saturday Sunday
Rest 6 miles 6 miles 5 miles 10 miles (half at race pace) 8 miles 3 hours (easy pace)

Nutrition

If you're training for an ultra-marathon, you're going to want
to eat a well balanced diet. Your diet should include whole grains,
lean proteins, healthy fats, & (obviously) fruits and vegetables.

What is an ultra-marathon?

An ultra-marathon is considered any race that is longer than a marathon.
It could be a 30 mile race, or it could be north of 100 miles!
